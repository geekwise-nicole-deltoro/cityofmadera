Council Member District 3

Council Member William Oliver
Madera City Council District 3

District 3 Map

Will Oliver is currently serving his first term in office as a member of the Madera City Council. Will was first elected to the City Council in 2014 and will serve a four year term through 2018.

Will served as Mayor Pro Tem during 2015. The Mayor Pro Tem position is rotated annually amongst the Council Members serving.

Members of the City Council also serve as Board Members of the Successor Agency to the Former Madera Redevelopment Agency and as Commissioners of the Housing Authority of the City of Madera.

Will is a third generation Maderan who has been a resident of Madera since 1990. Will attended the Sid Craig School of Business at California State University, Fresno where he earned a Bachelor of Science Degree in Business Administration.

Will currently works for the Fresno County Economic Development Corporation as a Business Support Specialist. As a Business Support Specialist he provides advocacy, outreach, and support services to area businesses. He has been there since 2013.

Will previously worked, from 2008-2012, as a staff assistant to United States Senator Barbara Boxer

Professional Affiliations / Other Public Service
In addition to their regular duties as Council Members, Members of the City Council are appointed to serve on other boards as representatives of the City Council. They are also active in community organizations. Mayor Pro Tem Oliver is currently serving on or has served in the past on the following boards and programs:

Central California Women’s Facility Citizens Advisory Board (alternate board member)
Community Action Partnership of Madera County (alternate board member)
Madera Vision 2025 Oversight and Implementation Committee (2005-2011)
City of Madera Community Development Block Grant Review and Advisory Committee (2010-2013)
Madera Sunrise Rotary Club
Friends of the Henry Madden Library Board of Directors
Professionals Reaching Out Guild for Ronald McDonald House Charities of the Central Valley

Awards and Special Achievements
2010 Jerry Noblett Award – Presented in recognition of his work on Vision Madera 2025.
